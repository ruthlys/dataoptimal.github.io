I am a microbial ecologist with a passion for data science. Currently, I am a Postdoctoral Fellow between the National Institute of Scientific Research (INRS) and the data analytics & visualization company Plotly. My research aims at finding microbial solutions to combat drought in agriculture for which I am combining Omic technologies with interactive data visualization tools in Dash for R and Python.

I conducted my graduate studies at the Technical University of Graz, Austria where I received a Master’s degree in Biotechnology for developing microbial consortia as sustainable alternative to pesticides in collaboration with Egypt’s first organic agricultural organization SEKEM. I then moved to the Netherlands to do my PhD at the Netherlands Institute of Ecology with in conjunction with Wageningen University where I studied the volatile language between microorganisms.
